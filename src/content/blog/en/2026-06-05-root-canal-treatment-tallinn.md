### What Is Root Canal Treatment and Why Might You Need It?

Root canal treatment — also known as endodontic therapy — is a procedure designed to remove infected or inflamed tissue from inside a tooth. Every tooth contains a soft inner core called the pulp, which houses nerves and blood vessels. When decay, trauma, or a cracked tooth allows bacteria to reach this tissue, infection can set in, causing significant pain and, left untreated, potentially spreading to the surrounding bone and jaw.

Common signs that you may need root canal treatment include:

- Persistent or throbbing toothache
- Prolonged sensitivity to heat or cold
- Darkening of the tooth
- Swelling or tenderness in the nearby gum
- A persistent pimple-like bump on the gum

The sooner the problem is addressed, the better the outcome. Delaying treatment rarely makes things easier — and it often makes them more complicated.

### How Root Canal Treatment Works

At Sonin Hambaravi in Tallinn, the procedure is carried out with precision and patient comfort as a priority. Dr Dmitri Sonin and his team use modern equipment and techniques that make the process significantly more straightforward than many patients expect.

Here is a simplified overview of what happens during treatment:

1. **Examination and imaging.** Digital X-rays help map the root canals and assess the extent of the infection.
2. **Local anaesthesia.** The area is numbed thoroughly so the procedure is as pain-free as possible.
3. **Access and cleaning.** A small opening is made in the crown of the tooth. The infected pulp is carefully removed, and the canals are cleaned and shaped.
4. **Disinfection.** The canals are irrigated with antibacterial solutions to eliminate remaining bacteria.
5. **Filling and sealing.** The cleaned canals are filled with a biocompatible material and sealed to prevent reinfection.
6. **Restoration.** In most cases, a crown is placed over the treated tooth to restore its strength and appearance.

Most treatments are completed in one or two appointments, depending on the complexity of the case.

### Is Root Canal Treatment Painful?

This is the question almost every patient asks. The honest answer: the procedure itself should not hurt. The infection is what causes pain, and once the area is properly anaesthetised, discomfort during treatment is minimal for the vast majority of patients. Some mild soreness in the days following the procedure is normal and typically manageable with over-the-counter pain relief.
