## Dental Implants Turkey vs Tallinn: A Cost Comparison by Dr Dmitri Sonin

When patients consider replacing a missing tooth with a dental implant, cost is frequently the deciding factor. Dental tourism — particularly to Turkey — has grown significantly among Baltic and Scandinavian patients. This article, prepared by **Dmitri Sonin** of Sonin Dental Clinic clinic in Tallinn, presents a straightforward numerical breakdown of both options, without editorial bias.

---

### The Base Procedure Cost: Implant + Crown

The core treatment — one titanium implant post plus a ceramic crown — carries the following typical price ranges:

| Location | Cost per tooth (implant + crown) |
|---|---|
| Tallinn (Sonin Hambaravi) | €1,200 – €1,800 |
| Turkey (Antalya / Istanbul) | €500 – €800 |

On procedure price alone, Turkey presents a difference of approximately €700–€1,000 per tooth. For patients requiring multiple implants, this gap widens proportionally.

---

### The True Cost of Traveling to Turkey

A procedure price is only one line item. Patients traveling from Tallinn to Turkey incur the following additional expenses:

**Flights (Tallinn → Antalya or Istanbul, return)**
Economy return flights typically range from **€200 to €400**, depending on season and booking lead time.

**Accommodation (5–7 nights)**
Most implant placements require two visits within one trip: the surgical placement and a follow-up fitting or assessment. A mid-range hotel for 5–7 nights costs approximately **€300–€600**.

**Food and local transfers**
Airport shuttles, taxis, and daily meals add a realistic **€150–€200** to the total.

**Total travel overhead: €650–€1,200**

Adding travel costs to the procedure price, the realistic all-in cost per tooth in Turkey falls in the range of **€1,150–€2,000** for a single-tooth implant trip.

---

### What Happens If Complications Arise?

Dental implant complications — while not common — do occur. Peri-implantitis, crown failure, osseointegration issues, or bite adjustments sometimes require a return visit to the treating clinic.

For a patient treated in Turkey, a return flight plus a short hotel stay represents an additional **€500–€800** per unplanned trip. This cost is difficult to predict in advance and is rarely covered by the original treatment package.

For patients treated at a Tallinn clinic, a follow-up appointment involves a 30-minute visit with no travel cost. **Sonin Hambaravi** backs its implant work with a **5-year guarantee**, meaning covered corrections are handled locally at no additional charge.

---

### Multi-Tooth Scenario: Running the Numbers

For a patient requiring **three implants**, the arithmetic changes:

**Turkey option (3 implants)**
- Procedure: 3 × €650 average = €1,950
- Travel overhead: €650–€1,200
- **Total: €2,600–€3,150**

**Tallinn option (3 implants)**
- Procedure: 3 × €1,500 average = €4,500
- No travel required
- **Total: €4,500**

The financial advantage of traveling abroad is more pronounced when the number of implants increases, as travel costs are distributed across more procedures.

---

### Factors That Do Not Appear in the Price

Several variables influence treatment outcomes but are not reflected in cost comparisons:

- **Implant brand and material** — Prices in Turkey vary widely based on which implant system is used. Confirming the exact brand and its international documentation before treatment is advisable.
- **Follow-up continuity** — Ongoing care is simpler when the treating clinic is accessible. Dr Dmitri Sonin notes that continuity of care — seeing the same clinician for placement, fitting, and follow-up — reduces the risk of miscommunication during the treatment sequence.
- **Language and documentation** — Treatment records, X-rays, and guarantee terms issued in Turkish require translation for any subsequent European provider.
- **Insurance compatibility** — Estonian health benefit schemes may partially cover implants performed domestically. Coverage for procedures abroad varies and requires individual verification.
