## Zirconia vs Metal-Ceramic Crown: Which to Choose in Tallinn? A Guide by Dr Dmitri Sonin

Maria, a 38-year-old teacher from Tallinn, came into our clinic with a cracked molar and a simple question: "Doctor, what kind of crown do I actually need?" She had done some research online but felt more confused than before. This is one of the most common situations Dr Dmitri Sonin encounters every week at Sonin Dental Clinic — and the answer, while not one-size-fits-all, is clearer than most patients expect.

---

### Why the Material of Your Crown Matters More Than You Think

A dental crown is not just a cap — it becomes a functional part of your bite for the next 10 to 20 years. The material determines how your crown looks, how it behaves under chewing pressure, how the surrounding gum tissue responds, and ultimately how long it lasts.

The two most common crown types offered in Tallinn today are **metal-ceramic (porcelain-fused-to-metal, or PFM)** and **full zirconia**. Each has a distinct set of properties that make it better suited for different patients and different teeth.

---

### Metal-Ceramic Crowns: Reliable, Affordable, but with Limitations

Metal-ceramic crowns have been the standard in dentistry for over 40 years — and for good reason. They are strong, predictable, and cost-effective. In Tallinn, a metal-ceramic crown typically ranges from **€180 to €320**, depending on the laboratory and the tooth location.

**However, there are real drawbacks:**

- A visible **dark grey line** at the gum margin, which becomes more noticeable as gums naturally recede with age
- The porcelain layer can **chip or fracture** under heavy biting forces
- Metal alloys can sometimes trigger **sensitivity or minor allergic reactions** in susceptible patients
- Less light transmission means the crown never fully mimics the natural translucency of a real tooth

For patients who need a crown on a back molar and are working within a budget, metal-ceramic remains a solid, proven option. But if the tooth is visible when you smile, the story changes.

---

### Zirconia Crowns: The Modern Standard for Aesthetics and Strength

Zirconia (zirconium dioxide) is a high-performance ceramic that has transformed restorative dentistry over the past decade. Dr Dmitri Sonin explains it to patients this way: *"Zirconia gives you the strength of metal with the appearance of a natural tooth — and no dark lines, ever."*

**Key advantages of zirconia:**

- **No metal substructure** — the entire crown is ceramic, so there is no grey line at the gumline
- **Exceptional strength** — modern zirconia crowns withstand forces of over **900 MPa**, making them suitable even for molars
- **Biocompatible** — gum tissue responds well, with lower rates of inflammation compared to metal alloys
- **Lifelike appearance** — high-translucency zirconia can closely replicate the natural depth and colour of enamel

In Tallinn, zirconia crowns typically cost between **€350 and €600**. The higher price reflects both material quality and laboratory precision.

### So Which Crown Should You Choose? A Practical Decision Guide

Here is the honest framework Dmitri Sonin uses with every patient during consultation:

| Factor | Metal-Ceramic | Zirconia |
|---|---|---|
| Budget | Lower cost | Higher investment |
| Tooth position | Back teeth (less visible) | Front or visible teeth |
| Gum aesthetics | Dark line possible | Clean, natural margin |
| Bruxism (grinding) | Risk of porcelain chipping | Highly resistant |
| Allergies to metal | Check alloy composition | No metal, fully safe |
| Longevity | 10–15 years average | 15–20+ years with care |

**When to see your dentist immediately:** If you already have a crown and notice **pain on biting, sensitivity to temperature, a visible crack, or a dark shadow forming at the gum**, do not wait. These are signs that either the crown has failed or the tooth underneath requires attention — delays can result in tooth loss.

---

### Key Takeaways Before Your Appointment

- There is no universally "best" crown — the right choice depends on your tooth's location, your bite, your aesthetics goals, and your budget
- Zirconia offers superior aesthetics and longevity; metal-ceramic remains a dependable budget option for non-visible teeth
- A well-made crown of either type, combined with good oral hygiene, can last well over a decade
- Proper diagnosis — including X-rays and bite analysis — is essential before any crown is placed
