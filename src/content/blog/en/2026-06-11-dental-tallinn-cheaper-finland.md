## Dental Treatment in Tallinn vs Finland: A Cost Analysis by Dr Dmitri Sonin

If you live in Finland and need a dental implant, you have already seen the price tag. A single implant with a crown at a Finnish clinic typically runs between **2,500 € and 4,000 €** per tooth. That figure alone sends thousands of Finnish patients across the Gulf of Finland each year. This article breaks down the actual numbers — including the less obvious ones — so you can make an informed financial decision.

---

### What Does an Implant Actually Cost in Tallinn?

At **Sonin Hambaravi** in Tallinn, a full implant and crown procedure is priced at **1,200 – 1,800 €** per tooth, depending on the implant system used and any preparatory work required (such as bone grafting or sinus lift). The price includes the surgical placement, the healing abutment, the ceramic crown, and follow-up check appointments.

The ferry from Helsinki to Tallinn takes approximately **2.5 hours**, with return tickets typically ranging from **40 – 120 €** depending on the season and operator. For a Finnish patient, the total cost of one implant in Tallinn — travel included — generally remains within the range of **1,300 – 1,950 €**. Compared to the Finnish baseline of 2,500 – 4,000 €, the numeric difference is substantial.

---

### How Does Tallinn Compare to Dental Tourism in Turkey?

Turkey — specifically Antalya and Istanbul — is frequently cited as a lower-cost destination for dental implants. The numbers are as follows:

| Cost Item | Estimated Range |
|---|---|
| Implant + crown (Turkey) | 500 – 800 € per tooth |
| Return flights (Tallinn–Antalya) | 200 – 400 € |
| Hotel, 5–7 nights | 300 – 600 € |
| Food and local transfers | 150 – 200 € |
| **Total, single implant (Turkey)** | **~1,150 – 2,000 €** |

At first glance, the Turkey total overlaps with the Tallinn price range. However, implant treatment is rarely completed in a single visit. If a complication arises — infection, implant failure, crown misfit — a return trip to Turkey adds another **500 – 800 €** in transport and accommodation alone, before any clinical costs are counted.

Dmitri Sonin notes that a meaningful share of patients arriving at Sonin Hambaravi are, in fact, seeking corrections or continuations of work started elsewhere. This is not an argument against any particular destination — it is simply a variable that belongs in the cost calculation.

---

### The Factor Most Patients Overlook: Guarantee and Follow-Up

A dental implant is not a one-time transaction. The long-term outcome depends on monitoring, occasional adjustments, and prompt response to early signs of complications. These touchpoints have a cost — either in travel or in local availability.

At **Sonin Hambaravi**, implants and prosthetic work carry a **5-year written guarantee**. A follow-up check takes approximately **30 minutes** and requires no additional travel for patients in the Tallinn–Helsinki corridor. For Finnish patients, a follow-up visit can be scheduled around an existing trip to Tallinn, or combined with a short break — adding minimal incremental cost.

For a patient who has had work done in a country requiring a 3–4 hour flight, each follow-up or complication visit resets the cost calculation significantly.

### A Straightforward Numeric Summary

| Scenario | Estimated Total (1 implant) |
|---|---|
| Finland (local clinic) | 2,500 – 4,000 € |
| Turkey (all-in, no complications) | 1,150 – 2,000 € |
| Turkey (one return complication visit) | 1,650 – 2,800 € |
| Tallinn — Sonin Hambaravi (incl. Helsinki ferry) | 1,300 – 1,950 € |

These are ranges, not guarantees. Individual cases vary. What the table does show is that the cost advantage of travelling further abroad narrows considerably once logistics are fully accounted for.
