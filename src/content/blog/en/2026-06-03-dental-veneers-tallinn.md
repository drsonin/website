### What Are Dental Veneers?

Dental veneers are ultra-thin shells — typically made from porcelain or composite resin — that are custom-crafted and bonded to the front surface of your teeth. They are designed to correct a wide range of cosmetic concerns, including:

- Chipped or cracked teeth
- Discolouration that doesn't respond to whitening
- Gaps between teeth
- Slightly misaligned or uneven teeth
- Worn-down enamel

The result is a beautifully uniform, natural-looking smile that feels completely your own. Veneers are one of the most popular cosmetic dentistry treatments in Estonia, and for good reason — they offer transformative results with minimal invasiveness.

### The Veneer Process at Sonin Hambaravi

Getting dental veneers is a straightforward, multi-step process that typically unfolds over two to three visits:

**1. Consultation and Planning**
Your journey begins with a comprehensive consultation. During this visit, Dmitri Sonin examines your teeth, discusses your smile goals, and determines whether veneers are the right treatment for you. Digital imaging and smile simulations may be used to help you visualise your result before any work begins.

**2. Tooth Preparation**
A small, minimal amount of enamel is gently removed from the tooth surface to ensure the veneer fits seamlessly and looks natural. Local anaesthesia is used to keep you comfortable throughout.

**3. Custom Fabrication**
Impressions of your teeth are sent to a trusted dental laboratory, where your veneers are carefully crafted to match the shape, size, and shade agreed upon during your consultation. Temporary veneers may be placed in the meantime.

**4. Bonding and Final Fitting**
Once your permanent veneers arrive, they are checked for fit and colour, then securely bonded to your teeth using a specialised dental adhesive. The final result is polished and refined to perfection.

### Porcelain vs. Composite Veneers: Which Is Right for You?

Both options have their advantages, and the best choice depends on your individual needs and budget.

**Porcelain veneers** are highly durable, stain-resistant, and offer the most lifelike appearance. They typically last 10–15 years or longer with proper care.

**Composite veneers** can often be completed in a single visit, making them a faster and more affordable alternative. While they may not last quite as long as porcelain, they can still produce excellent aesthetic results and are easier to repair if damaged.

During your consultation, Dr Dmitri Sonin will walk you through both options and help you make an informed decision that suits your lifestyle and smile goals.
