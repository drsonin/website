## Dental Implant Cost in Tallinn: A Complete Guide by Dr Dmitri Sonin

### What Is the Average Cost of a Dental Implant in Tallinn?

Dental implant prices in Tallinn typically range from €800 to €1,800 per implant, depending on the complexity of the case, the implant system used, and whether additional procedures such as bone grafting are required. This is significantly more affordable than in Finland or Sweden, where a single implant can cost €2,500 or more, without any compromise in the standard of care.

At Sonin Dental Clinic, pricing is transparent and broken down clearly so patients always know what they're paying for — from the titanium implant fixture itself to the abutment and the final ceramic crown.

### What's Included in the Implant Price?

Many clinics advertise a low headline price that excludes essential components. At Sonin Dental Clinic, a full implant treatment package typically includes:

- **Initial consultation and digital X-rays or 3D CBCT scan**
- **The implant fixture** (the titanium post placed into the jawbone)
- **The abutment** (the connector between implant and crown)
- **The porcelain or zirconia crown** (the visible tooth)
- **Follow-up appointments** during the healing period

It's important to ask any clinic for a fully itemised quote before committing. Hidden costs for scans, temporary crowns, or check-ups can significantly increase the final bill.

### Factors That Affect Dental Implant Cost

Several variables influence the total cost of your implant treatment:

**Bone grafting:** If you have experienced bone loss in the jaw — common after long-term tooth absence — a bone graft may be needed before implant placement. This adds both time and cost to treatment.

**Implant brand and system:** Premium implant systems from manufacturers such as Straumann or Nobel Biocare carry a higher price point, but they are backed by decades of clinical research and long-term reliability data.

**Number of implants:** Patients missing multiple teeth may benefit from implant-supported bridges or All-on-4 solutions, which reduce the cost per tooth considerably.

**Complexity of extraction:** If the damaged tooth is still present and requires removal, this is typically priced separately.

### How Long Do Dental Implants Last?

With proper oral hygiene and regular check-ups, dental implants can last a lifetime. Unlike dentures or bridges, they do not rely on neighbouring teeth for support and they preserve the jawbone by stimulating natural bone tissue — just as a natural tooth root does.

Dr Dmitri Sonin recommends patients treat implants the same as natural teeth: brush twice daily, floss regularly, and attend routine dental check-ups every six months.
