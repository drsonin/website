### What Are Ceramic Inlays?

A ceramic inlay is a custom-made restoration designed to repair a tooth that has been damaged by decay or minor fractures. Unlike a standard filling that is applied directly in the mouth, an inlay is fabricated in a dental laboratory and then bonded precisely into the prepared cavity of your tooth.

Ceramic inlays are an ideal middle ground between a simple filling and a full crown. They preserve more of your natural tooth structure, fit seamlessly into the contours of your bite, and blend in beautifully with the surrounding enamel thanks to their tooth-coloured porcelain finish.

### Why Choose Ceramic Over Other Materials?

Ceramic restorations offer several important advantages over traditional amalgam or composite fillings:

- **Aesthetics** — Ceramic closely mimics the natural translucency and colour of tooth enamel, making the restoration virtually invisible.
- **Durability** — High-quality dental porcelain is highly resistant to wear and can last well over a decade with proper care.
- **Biocompatibility** — Ceramic is non-metallic and does not cause sensitivity reactions, making it an excellent choice for patients with metal sensitivities.
- **Stability** — Unlike composite fillings, ceramic does not shrink or expand with temperature changes, reducing the risk of micro-leakage over time.
- **Tooth preservation** — Inlays require minimal removal of healthy tooth structure compared to full crowns.

For patients in Tallinn who value both function and appearance, ceramic inlays represent one of the most advanced and reliable options available today.

### The Treatment Process at Sonin Hambaravi

Dr Dmitri Sonin approaches every restoration with careful planning and a commitment to long-term patient satisfaction. The ceramic inlay process typically involves two appointments.

During the **first visit**, the tooth is gently prepared by removing any decay or damaged material. Precise digital or physical impressions are taken to capture the exact dimensions of the cavity. A temporary filling is placed to protect the tooth while the permanent inlay is being crafted in the laboratory.

At the **second visit**, Dr Sonin removes the temporary filling, checks the fit and colour match of the finished inlay, and bonds it firmly into place using a strong dental adhesive. The result is a seamless, fully functional restoration that feels completely natural when you bite and chew.

### Who Is a Good Candidate?

Ceramic inlays are suitable for most adults who have moderate tooth damage or cavities that are too large for a standard filling yet do not require a full crown. They are particularly recommended for:

- Patients who want a metal-free, aesthetically pleasing restoration
- Those replacing old, deteriorating amalgam fillings
- Anyone seeking a long-term investment in their dental health
- Patients with teeth that experience significant chewing pressure (particularly premolars and molars)

During your consultation, Dmitri Sonin will examine your teeth, discuss your goals, and recommend the most appropriate treatment plan for your individual needs.

### Caring for Your Ceramic Inlay

With proper oral hygiene, a ceramic inlay can last 10 to 15 years or even longer. To protect your restoration, it is recommended to:

- Brush twice daily with a soft-bristled toothbrush
- Floss regularly, including around the restored tooth
- Attend routine dental check-ups at least twice a year
- Avoid biting on extremely hard objects, such as ice or hard candy

Ceramic restorations are stain-resistant, but maintaining good overall oral hygiene ensures both the inlay and the surrounding teeth remain in excellent condition.
