### What Is Professional Teeth Cleaning?

Professional teeth cleaning, also known as dental prophylaxis or hygiene treatment, is a thorough procedure performed by a trained dental hygienist or dentist. It removes hardened deposits (tartar) that cannot be eliminated by a toothbrush alone, as well as surface stains and bacterial plaque from areas that are difficult to reach at home.

A standard session typically includes:

- **Scaling** — removing tartar above and below the gumline
- **Polishing** — buffing the tooth surfaces to remove stains and smooth enamel
- **Flossing and rinsing** — clearing residue from between the teeth
- **Fluoride treatment** — optional strengthening step recommended for many patients

The entire appointment usually takes between 45 and 90 minutes, depending on the condition of your teeth and gums.

### The Health Benefits You Should Not Ignore

Many people think of teeth cleaning purely as a cosmetic procedure. In reality, it is one of the most important preventive health steps you can take. Regular professional cleaning:

- **Prevents gum disease** — Periodontal disease begins with plaque build-up. Early removal dramatically reduces your risk.
- **Reduces the risk of tooth loss** — Healthy gums and clean tooth surfaces are the foundation of a lasting smile.
- **Supports overall health** — Research links poor oral hygiene to heart disease, diabetes complications and other systemic conditions.
- **Detects problems early** — During your cleaning appointment, the dentist can spot cavities, cracks or early signs of disease before they become costly problems.

Most dental professionals recommend a professional cleaning every six months, though patients with a history of gum disease may benefit from more frequent visits.
