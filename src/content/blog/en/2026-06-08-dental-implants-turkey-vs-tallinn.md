## Dental Implants: Turkey vs Tallinn Cost Comparison — An Analysis by Dr Dmitri Sonin

Every year, thousands of Europeans weigh up whether to have dental implants fitted abroad or at home. The numbers circulating online can be striking. This breakdown, prepared by **Dmitri Sonin** of Sonin Hambaravi dental clinic in Tallinn, lays out the verified figures for both destinations so you can make an informed decision.

---

### What Does One Implant + Crown Actually Cost?

The baseline price for a single dental implant with a ceramic crown varies considerably between Estonia and Turkey.

| Location | Price per implant + crown |
|---|---|
| Tallinn (Sonin Hambaravi) | €1,200 – €1,800 |
| Antalya / Istanbul, Turkey | €500 – €800 |

On paper, the Turkish price is 50–70% lower. For a patient needing four implants, the clinic price difference alone could reach €4,000–€5,600. That figure, however, does not represent the total cost of treatment abroad.

---

### The Real Cost of Travelling to Turkey for Implants

Dental implant treatment typically requires at least two visits: one for implant placement and one for fitting the final crown, usually separated by 3–6 months of healing. Many clinics in Turkey consolidate this into one longer stay, which means additional time away from work and higher travel expenses.

**Estimated travel and accommodation costs for one trip:**

- Return flights, Tallinn → Antalya or Istanbul: **€200 – €400**
- Hotel accommodation (5–7 nights): **€300 – €600**
- Food, local transfers, and incidentals: **€150 – €200**

**Total additional trip cost: approximately €650 – €1,200**

Adding these to the clinic price brings the realistic cost to **€1,150 – €2,000** for a single implant treated abroad over one consolidated trip. For multiple implants, the per-unit travel cost decreases, which is where the financial case for dental tourism becomes more apparent.

---

### The Cost of Complications and Follow-Up Care

This is the figure most cost comparisons omit. Dental implants carry a documented complication rate of 5–10% over five years, regardless of where they are placed. Common issues include peri-implantitis (gum infection around the implant), crown fractures, and implant failure requiring removal.

If a complication arises after treatment in Turkey, the patient faces a choice: manage it locally in Tallinn, often requiring a clinician unfamiliar with the original work, or return to the treating clinic abroad.

**Estimated cost of one unplanned return trip to Turkey:**

- Flights + hotel + transfers: **€500 – €800**

This single unplanned trip can eliminate the majority of savings made on a straightforward case. If the complication requires retreatment, materials, or a replacement implant, costs increase further and must be negotiated remotely with the original provider.
