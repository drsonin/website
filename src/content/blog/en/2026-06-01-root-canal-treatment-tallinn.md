### What Is Root Canal Treatment?

Root canal treatment, also known as endodontic therapy, is a dental procedure designed to save a tooth that has become severely infected or damaged at its pulp — the soft tissue inside the tooth containing nerves and blood vessels. When bacteria reach this inner layer due to deep decay, a cracked tooth, or trauma, infection can spread quickly, causing significant pain and potentially leading to tooth loss if left untreated.

The treatment involves removing the infected pulp, thoroughly cleaning and shaping the root canals, and sealing them to prevent reinfection. In most cases, the tooth is then restored with a crown to protect it long-term.

### Signs You Might Need a Root Canal

Many patients arrive at our Tallinn clinic unsure whether their symptoms warrant a root canal. Common warning signs include:

- **Persistent toothache**, especially when chewing or applying pressure
- **Prolonged sensitivity** to hot or cold temperatures
- **Darkening or discolouration** of the tooth
- **Swollen or tender gums** near the affected tooth
- **A persistent pimple or bump** on the gums

It's important not to ignore these symptoms. Early intervention can mean the difference between saving and losing a tooth.

### What to Expect During Treatment at Sonin Hambaravi

At Sonin Hambaravi clinic, patient comfort is a top priority. Dr Dmitri Sonin uses the latest endodontic equipment and techniques to ensure precise, effective treatment with minimal discomfort.

Here's a general overview of the process:

1. **Initial examination and X-rays** — We assess the extent of infection and plan your treatment carefully.
2. **Local anaesthesia** — The area is thoroughly numbed so you feel little to no pain during the procedure.
3. **Pulp removal and canal cleaning** — Using specialised instruments, the infected tissue is removed and the canals are cleaned and shaped.
4. **Sealing the tooth** — The canals are filled with a biocompatible material called gutta-percha to prevent future infection.
5. **Restoration** — In a follow-up appointment, the tooth is typically fitted with a crown for full function and protection.

Most root canal treatments are completed in one to two appointments, depending on the complexity of the case.

### Is Root Canal Treatment Painful?

This is the question we hear most often — and the answer may surprise you. Thanks to effective local anaesthesia and modern techniques, most patients report that root canal treatment feels no more uncomfortable than having a standard filling. Any mild soreness in the days following treatment is typically manageable with over-the-counter pain relief.

The real discomfort is the infection itself. Root canal treatment is what *ends* the pain, not causes it.
