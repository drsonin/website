## Dental Tourism Hidden Costs: A Real Cost Calculation by Dr Dmitri Sonin

Every year, thousands of patients from Northern and Western Europe travel abroad for dental implants, drawn by prices that look significantly lower on paper. But how do the numbers actually compare once every expense is counted? Dr Dmitri Sonin, implantologist at Sonin Hambaravi clinic in Tallinn, has compiled a straightforward breakdown to help patients make an informed financial decision.

---

### The Headline Price: What Clinics Advertise

The most commonly quoted comparison is simple:

| Procedure | Tallinn (Sonin Hambaravi) | Turkey (Antalya / Istanbul) |
|---|---|---|
| Implant + crown (per tooth) | €1,200 – €1,800 | €500 – €800 |

On a single implant, the apparent saving ranges from **€400 to €1,300**. On three or four implants, that figure looks substantial. This is where most patients stop calculating — and where the real analysis should begin.

---

### Adding the Mandatory Travel Costs

Dental implant treatment is not a single appointment. It typically requires a minimum of **two visits**: one for implant placement and one for crown fitting, separated by a healing period of 3–6 months. Some patients travel for both; others coordinate the second visit remotely, which carries its own risks.

For a single combined trip (implant placement only, with the second stage managed locally or via a return trip), the unavoidable costs from Tallinn include:

| Cost Item | Low Estimate | High Estimate |
|---|---|---|
| Return flights (Tallinn–Antalya) | €200 | €400 |
| Hotel (5–7 nights) | €300 | €600 |
| Food and local transfers | €150 | €200 |
| **Travel subtotal** | **€650** | **€1,200** |

Adding these to the treatment cost of one implant + crown:

| Scenario | Turkey (low) | Turkey (high) |
|---|---|---|
| Implant + crown | €500 | €800 |
| Travel costs | €650 | €1,200 |
| **Total** | **€1,150** | **€2,000** |

The comparable total at Sonin Hambaravi in Tallinn: **€1,200 – €1,800**.

At this point, the financial gap has largely closed — or, in the higher-estimate scenario, reversed entirely.

---

### The Complication Variable

Dental implants carry a complication rate that no clinic, regardless of location, can reduce to zero. Industry data places implant failure and complication rates between 5% and 10% over a 10-year period, with early complications occurring in roughly 2–5% of cases.

If a complication arises after treatment abroad — infection, implant failure, crown misalignment — the patient faces a clear choice: return to the original clinic or seek corrective treatment locally.

**A single return trip for complications adds:**

- Flights: €200 – €400
- Accommodation: €150 – €300
- Any additional treatment costs: variable

Dmitri Sonin notes that the administrative complexity also increases: obtaining original X-rays, implant brand documentation, and treatment records from abroad introduces delays that can affect outcomes.

At Sonin Hambaravi, the **5-year guarantee** on implant work covers follow-up appointments and adjustments. A standard check takes approximately 30 minutes. There are no additional travel costs, no language barriers, and no documentation delays.

### Total Cost Comparison: Three-Implant Scenario

To illustrate the calculation at scale, here is a side-by-side for three implants — a common treatment volume:

| Cost Item | Tallinn | Turkey |
|---|---|---|
| Implant × 3 + crowns × 3 | €3,600 – €5,400 | €1,500 – €2,400 |
| Travel (one trip) | €0 | €650 – €1,200 |
| One complication return trip (if needed) | €0 | €350 – €700 |
| **Realistic total range** | **€3,600 – €5,400** | **€2,500 – €4,300** |

A saving of €0 to €2,900 remains — depending on whether complications occur and how many trips are required.

---

### What the Numbers Show

The difference between dental tourism and local treatment narrows significantly once travel is included. For one or two implants, the financial argument for travelling abroad weakens considerably. For larger treatment plans (four or more implants), a gap may remain, depending on individual circumstances.

Variables that affect the outcome include: whether two visits are needed, whether complications arise, the patient's available time, and the value placed on continuity of care.
