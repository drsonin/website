### Why Pediatric Dentistry Matters from an Early Age

Many parents underestimate the importance of baby teeth. Primary teeth are not simply placeholders — they guide speech development, support proper nutrition through chewing, and hold space for permanent teeth to grow in correctly. Untreated decay in baby teeth can lead to pain, infection, and even long-term alignment problems.

The World Health Organization recommends that children have their first dental visit by the age of one, or within six months of the first tooth appearing. Starting early helps children become comfortable with dental visits, reducing anxiety as they grow older. At Sonin Hambaravi, we make this first visit a welcoming milestone rather than a stressful event.

### Common Pediatric Dental Services We Offer

At Sonin Hambaravi in Tallinn, our team provides a comprehensive range of services tailored specifically to growing smiles:

**Cavity Prevention and Treatment**
Tooth decay is the most common chronic childhood disease, yet it is largely preventable. We use fissure sealants — thin protective coatings applied to the chewing surfaces of back teeth — to shield vulnerable areas from bacteria and food particles. When cavities do develop, we treat them gently and efficiently using modern, minimally invasive techniques.

**Orthodontic Monitoring**
We keep a close eye on jaw and bite development throughout childhood. Early identification of crowding, crossbites, or other alignment issues means we can refer for orthodontic assessment at exactly the right time, making treatment more effective and often shorter.

**Emergency Dental Care**
Children are active, and accidents happen. Whether it is a knocked-out tooth from a playground fall or a painful toothache on a school night, our Tallinn clinic is ready to help promptly and compassionately.

**Dental Anxiety Management**
We know that dental fear in children is real. Our calm, unhurried approach — combined with clear communication and a friendly environment — helps even the most nervous young patients relax. Dmitri Sonin and the team are trained to work at each child's own pace, building confidence visit by visit.

### Teaching Healthy Habits for Life

A significant part of pediatric dentistry is education. We coach children on proper brushing and flossing techniques, explain the role of diet in oral health, and motivate them to take ownership of their own smiles. When children understand the "why" behind dental care, they are far more likely to maintain good habits into adulthood.

Parents receive practical guidance too — from choosing the right toothbrush and toothpaste for their child's age to understanding how thumb-sucking or pacifier use may affect development.
