### What Is Root Canal Treatment and When Is It Needed?

Root canal treatment, also known as endodontic therapy, is a dental procedure used to save a tooth that has become severely infected or inflamed at its pulp — the soft tissue inside the tooth containing nerves and blood vessels.

You may need root canal treatment if you experience:

- Persistent or severe toothache
- Prolonged sensitivity to hot or cold temperatures
- Darkening or discolouration of a tooth
- Swelling or tenderness in the nearby gums
- A persistent pimple or abscess on the gum

Left untreated, an infected tooth can lead to more serious complications, including the spread of infection to surrounding teeth or even the jawbone. Early intervention is always the better choice.

### What to Expect During the Procedure

Many patients feel anxious about root canal treatment due to its outdated reputation for being painful. The reality today is quite different. With modern anaesthesia and advanced techniques, the procedure is generally no more uncomfortable than having a standard filling placed.

At Sonin Hambaravi, the process typically involves:

1. **Diagnosis and X-rays** – We carefully assess the extent of the infection using digital imaging.
2. **Local anaesthesia** – The area is fully numbed to ensure your comfort throughout.
3. **Pulp removal** – The infected or inflamed pulp is gently removed from the root canals.
4. **Cleaning and shaping** – The canals are meticulously cleaned, shaped, and disinfected.
5. **Filling and sealing** – The canals are filled with a biocompatible material and sealed to prevent re-infection.
6. **Restoration** – A crown or filling is placed to restore the tooth's function and appearance.

Most cases are completed in one or two appointments, depending on the complexity of the infection.
