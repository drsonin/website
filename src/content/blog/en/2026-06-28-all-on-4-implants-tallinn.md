## All-on-4 Dental Implants in Tallinn: A Complete Guide by Dr Dmitri Sonin

### What Are All-on-4 Dental Implants?

All-on-4 is a full-arch tooth replacement technique that uses just four strategically placed dental implants to support an entire set of fixed prosthetic teeth. Unlike traditional implants, which require one implant per missing tooth, the All-on-4 method is designed to maximise the use of available bone — meaning that many patients who have been told they lack sufficient bone density can still be treated without the need for bone grafting.

The two rear implants are angled at up to 45 degrees, providing a strong and stable foundation even in areas where bone volume is reduced. The result is a permanent, non-removable set of teeth that look, feel, and function like natural ones.

### The All-on-4 Process at Sonin Dental Clinic

At Sonin Dental Clinic, every All-on-4 journey begins with a thorough consultation and full digital assessment, including 3D cone beam CT scanning. This detailed imaging allows Dr Dmitri Sonin and his team to plan each implant placement with precision, ensuring optimal stability and aesthetics before a single incision is made.

The surgical procedure itself is typically completed in a single day. Under local anaesthesia or sedation, the four implants are placed and a temporary fixed bridge is attached immediately — so patients leave the clinic with a functional set of teeth on the same day as their surgery.

After a healing period of several months, during which the implants integrate with the jawbone in a process called osseointegration, the temporary bridge is replaced with the final, custom-made prosthesis. The final result is highly durable, easy to clean, and indistinguishable from natural teeth in both appearance and feel.

### Who Is a Good Candidate?

All-on-4 implants are suitable for adults who have lost most or all of their teeth in one or both jaws, or those facing extensive tooth loss due to decay, gum disease, or injury. Good overall health and a commitment to oral hygiene and follow-up care are the key requirements. Even patients with some degree of bone loss are often eligible, which is one of the greatest advantages of this technique.

Dmitri Sonin takes time during the initial consultation to assess each patient individually, reviewing medical history, imaging results, and treatment goals before recommending any course of action.

### All-on-4 vs Other Full-Arch Solutions

Patients sometimes ask how All-on-4 compares with All-on-6 implants or traditional implant-supported dentures. While All-on-6 uses two additional implants for extra support — which may be recommended in certain clinical situations — All-on-4 remains the gold standard for most full-arch cases due to its predictability, shorter treatment timeline, and the avoidance of bone grafts in the majority of patients.

Removable implant-supported dentures are another option, but they do not offer the same level of stability or comfort as a fixed All-on-4 bridge, and many patients find the fixed solution far preferable for daily life.
