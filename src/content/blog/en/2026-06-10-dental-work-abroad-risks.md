## Dental Work Abroad: Risks, Guarantees and the Real Numbers — An Analysis by Dr Dmitri Sonin

Every year, thousands of patients across Northern Europe weigh the idea of combining a holiday with dental treatment. The math appears straightforward at first glance: lower prices abroad, sunshine included. But the full financial picture is rarely that simple. Below is a factual cost breakdown and a clear-eyed look at what guarantees actually mean in practice.

---

### The Headline Prices: What You See First

For one of the most common procedures — a single dental implant with a ceramic crown — published prices vary significantly by location:

- **Tallinn (Sonin Hambaravi clinic):** approximately **1,200–1,800 €** per tooth
- **Antalya or Istanbul, Turkey:** approximately **500–800 €** per tooth

On a single implant, the apparent saving is roughly **700–1,000 €**. On paper, this looks compelling.

---

### The Real Cost of Traveling for Treatment

Dental treatment abroad involves logistics that carry their own price tags. Using a Tallinn–Antalya trip as a realistic example:

| Expense | Estimated Cost |
|---|---|
| Return flights (Tallinn–Antalya) | 200–400 € |
| Hotel (5–7 nights, standard) | 300–600 € |
| Food and local transfers | 150–200 € |
| **Travel subtotal** | **650–1,200 €** |

Add the implant and crown cost at the lower Turkish estimate (500 €), and the total floor for one tooth abroad reaches approximately **1,150–2,000 €** — before accounting for any complications.

---

### The Complication Factor: An Underestimated Variable

Implant complications — infection, osseointegration failure, crown misalignment, or bite issues — occur across all geographies. The clinical question is not *whether* complications can happen, but *what they cost when they do*.

When treatment is performed locally in Tallinn, a follow-up appointment is a 30-minute visit with no additional travel cost. When treatment is performed abroad, addressing a complication typically requires one of the following:

- **A return trip to the original clinic** in Turkey: flights, accommodation, and transfers again — realistically **500–800 € additional spend**
- **Corrective treatment in Tallinn** from a different clinic, which may or may not be covered by the original provider's guarantee

This single unplanned return journey can eliminate the entire price difference between treatment locations.

---

### What a Guarantee Actually Covers

Dmitri Sonin notes that the term "guarantee" in dentistry deserves careful examination before a patient commits to any provider.

Key questions to ask any dental clinic — local or abroad:

1. **Duration:** How many years does the guarantee cover?
2. **Coverage scope:** Does it include the implant, the crown, and the surgical work separately?
3. **Claim process:** How do you make a claim if you live in a different country?
4. **Cost of claim:** Does exercising the guarantee require you to travel back?

At Sonin Hambaravi, the standard guarantee on implant treatment is **5 years**, with follow-up checks conducted locally in Tallinn. The practical value of a local guarantee is that it can be exercised without additional expense or logistical planning.

A written guarantee from a clinic 3,000 kilometres away has a different practical value — not a lesser *legal* value necessarily, but a meaningfully higher *cost to exercise*.

---

### Summarising the Numbers: One Tooth, Two Scenarios

| Scenario | Best Case | Realistic Case (with one complication) |
|---|---|---|
| Treatment in Turkey + travel | 1,150 € | 1,650–2,800 € |
| Treatment in Tallinn | 1,200–1,800 € | 1,200–1,800 € |

The gap between the two scenarios narrows significantly once travel, accommodation, and complication probability are included in the calculation. Whether that remaining gap justifies the logistical and clinical uncertainty is a decision each patient must make based on their own circumstances.

A complete picture is always a better basis for a financial decision than a headline price.
