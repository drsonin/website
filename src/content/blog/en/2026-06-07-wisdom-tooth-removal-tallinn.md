### What Are Wisdom Teeth and Why Do They Cause Problems?

Wisdom teeth, or third molars, are the last teeth to erupt — typically between the ages of 17 and 25. While some people never experience any issues with them, many find that these teeth simply don't have enough room to grow properly. When a wisdom tooth is partially or fully trapped beneath the gum line, it is described as **impacted**.

Impacted or misaligned wisdom teeth can cause a range of problems, including:

- Persistent pain and pressure in the jaw
- Infection or inflammation of the surrounding gum tissue
- Damage to neighbouring teeth
- The formation of cysts or abscesses
- Difficulty opening the mouth or chewing

In many cases, early removal is the safest and most effective solution.

### When Is Wisdom Tooth Removal Necessary?

Not every wisdom tooth needs to be extracted. During your consultation at Sonin Hambaravi, Dr Dmitri Sonin will carry out a thorough clinical examination and review your dental X-rays to assess the position, angle, and condition of your wisdom teeth. Removal is typically recommended when:

- The tooth is impacted or growing at an awkward angle
- There is recurrent infection or pain around the tooth
- The tooth is contributing to overcrowding
- Decay or gum disease has developed and cannot be adequately treated

If extraction is not immediately necessary, Dr Sonin will discuss monitoring options and advise on the best course of action for your individual situation.

### What to Expect During the Procedure

Wisdom tooth removal in Tallinn at Sonin Hambaravi is carried out under local anaesthesia, ensuring you remain comfortable throughout. For more complex cases — such as deeply impacted teeth or patients with significant dental anxiety — sedation options can also be discussed.

The procedure itself typically takes between 20 and 60 minutes, depending on the complexity of the extraction. Simple extractions, where the tooth has fully erupted, are completed quickly. Surgical extractions, involving a small incision in the gum, are required for impacted teeth and require a little more time and care.

Throughout the process, our clinical team prioritises clear communication, so you always know what to expect at each stage.

### Recovery: What Happens After Removal?

Most patients recover fully within one to two weeks. During the first few days, some swelling, mild discomfort, and light bleeding are entirely normal. To support a smooth recovery, Dmitri Sonin and our team will provide you with detailed aftercare instructions, which generally include:

- Applying a cold compress to reduce swelling
- Eating soft foods for the first few days
- Avoiding smoking, alcohol, and straws
- Keeping the area clean with gentle saltwater rinses
- Taking prescribed or over-the-counter pain relief as directed

Most patients find they can return to normal daily activities within two to three days.
