## Dental Implant Cost in Tallinn vs Helsinki: What Dr Dmitri Sonin Wants You to Know

If you're a Finnish patient considering dental implants, you've likely already noticed the significant price difference between dental care at home and across the Gulf of Finland. Every year, thousands of Finns travel to Tallinn specifically for dental treatment — and for good reason. In this article, Dr Dmitri Sonin of Sonin Dental Clinic clinic breaks down exactly what you can expect to pay, and why the journey to Estonia makes financial sense.

---

### The Numbers: How Much Do Dental Implants Cost?

Let's start with the most important question: the actual cost comparison.

In **Helsinki**, a single dental implant — including the implant fixture, abutment, and crown — typically ranges from **€2,500 to €4,000** or more, depending on the clinic and materials used. When multiple implants are needed, costs can quickly climb into the tens of thousands of euros.

In **Tallinn**, the same high-quality, full implant treatment at a reputable clinic like Sonin Hambaravi is generally priced between **€900 and €1,600 per implant**. That's a saving of roughly 50–60% per tooth — without any compromise in quality.

---

### Why Is Dental Care So Much Cheaper in Tallinn?

Many Finnish patients ask this question, and it's a fair one. Lower prices naturally raise concerns about quality. The answer, however, lies in economics rather than standards.

Estonia has a significantly lower cost of living and operating costs compared to Finland. Clinic rent, staff salaries, and administrative overheads are all considerably lower in Tallinn. This means dental clinics can charge less while still investing in the same premium implant systems — such as Nobel Biocare or Straumann — that leading Finnish clinics use.

Dental education and clinical standards in Estonia also meet EU regulations, so patients receive care that aligns with European healthcare norms. Dmitri Sonin and the team at Sonin Hambaravi hold internationally recognised qualifications and regularly participate in professional development to stay current with the latest implantology techniques.

### Travel Costs: Does the Trip Still Make Sense?

Tallinn is just a 2.5-hour ferry ride from Helsinki, with multiple daily crossings operated by Tallink and Viking Line. Return tickets often cost as little as €30–€60 per person, making the trip both easy and affordable.

For a straightforward single implant, Finnish patients typically need two to three visits over the course of several months — one for implant placement and one for crown fitting, with a check-up in between. Even accounting for ferry tickets and a night's accommodation if needed, the total savings remain substantial.

For patients requiring full-arch restorations or multiple implants, the financial difference becomes even more compelling. It's not unusual for a Finnish patient to save **€5,000 to €10,000** on a comprehensive treatment plan.
