## Loose or Painful Dental Implant in Tallinn? Dr Dmitri Sonin Explains What to Do

You woke up this morning and something felt off — your dental implant is moving slightly, or there's a dull ache around it that wasn't there last week. Maybe you're pressing your tongue against it nervously, wondering if you're imagining things. You're not. A loose or painful dental implant is a signal your body is sending you, and it's one you should take seriously — but not panic over.

---

### Why Does a Dental Implant Become Loose or Painful?

Understanding the cause is the first step toward the right solution. "Not all implant pain means the same thing," says **Dmitri Sonin**, implant dentist at Sonin Dental Clinic clinic in Tallinn. "The cause determines everything — the urgency, the treatment, and the outcome."

Here are the most common reasons an implant may feel loose or uncomfortable:

- **Peri-implantitis** — a bacterial infection of the gum and bone tissue surrounding the implant. It's the most frequent culprit, affecting roughly 10–20% of implants over time. It causes bone loss, which literally reduces the foundation holding your implant in place.
- **Failed osseointegration** — occasionally, the titanium post never fully fuses with the jawbone. This is more common in smokers, diabetics, or patients with compromised bone density, and typically becomes apparent within the first 3–6 months after placement.
- **Loose abutment or crown** — sometimes it's not the implant itself that's moving, but the connector piece (abutment) or the crown on top. This is a simpler fix and should not be ignored, as an uneven bite puts extra stress on surrounding bone.
- **Excessive bite force or bruxism** — grinding your teeth at night generates forces of up to 250 kg per square centimetre, which can gradually destabilise even a well-integrated implant.
- **Trauma** — a knock to the face or biting something hard unexpectedly can fracture components or disturb tissue.

---

### What to Do Right Now: Step-by-Step

1. **Stop chewing on that side.** Avoid hard, crunchy, or sticky foods immediately to prevent further mechanical stress.
2. **Don't wiggle or test the implant.** It's tempting, but repeated movement can worsen bone irritation.
3. **Maintain gentle oral hygiene.** Continue brushing carefully around the area and rinse with a mild saline solution or chlorhexidine 0.12% mouthwash twice daily to reduce bacterial load.
4. **Take OTC pain relief if needed.** Ibuprofen (400 mg, every 6–8 hours with food) reduces both pain and localised inflammation while you wait for your appointment.
5. **Call your dental clinic within 24–48 hours.** A loose implant is not something to "wait and see" about for weeks.

---

### When to See a Dentist Immediately — Same Day

Some symptoms cannot wait. Go to your dentist the same day if you experience:

- **Visible swelling of the gum or cheek** that is growing or spreading
- **Pus or an unpleasant taste** coming from around the implant
- **Fever above 38°C** combined with oral pain
- **Significant mobility** — the implant moving more than 1 mm in any direction
- **Sudden inability to close your bite properly**

These signs suggest active infection or acute structural failure, both of which can lead to irreversible bone loss if treatment is delayed beyond 24–72 hours. **Dr Dmitri Sonin** stresses: "In implantology, time really does matter. A problem caught on day one is almost always easier and less costly to solve than the same problem discovered three weeks later."

---

### Prevention and Key Takeaways

The good news: the vast majority of implant complications are preventable.

- **Professional cleaning every 6 months** — implants need specialised instruments (plastic or titanium scalers) to clean safely without surface damage.
- **Wear a night guard if you grind** — this single step can extend implant longevity by years.
- **Quit smoking** — smokers have a 2–3× higher implant failure rate compared to non-smokers.
- **Control systemic conditions** — well-managed blood sugar in diabetic patients dramatically improves healing and integration.
- **Don't skip follow-up X-rays** — annual radiographs allow your dentist to spot early bone loss before you feel any symptoms.
