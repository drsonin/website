## Immediate Dental Implants in Tallinn: What Dr Dmitri Sonin Wants You to Know

### What Are Immediate Dental Implants?

An immediate dental implant — sometimes called a same-day implant — is placed directly into the extraction socket on the very same day the damaged or failing tooth is removed. Unlike conventional implants, which require the patient to wait several months for the socket to heal before placement, immediate implants compress the entire process into one streamlined appointment. A temporary crown is often fitted the same day, meaning patients leave the clinic with both an implant and a natural-looking tooth already in place.

This approach is not suitable for every patient, but when the clinical conditions are right, it delivers outstanding results in a fraction of the time.

### How the Procedure Works at Sonin Dental Clinic

At Sonin Dental Clinic, the journey begins with a thorough consultation and a 3D cone beam CT scan to assess bone density, volume, and the precise anatomy of the implant site. This imaging allows the clinical team to plan the procedure with exceptional accuracy before a single instrument is picked up.

On the day of treatment, the failing tooth is carefully extracted, the socket is cleaned and prepared, and a titanium implant is positioned with precise torque to achieve immediate stability — what clinicians call primary stability. This is the critical factor that makes same-day loading possible. Once stability is confirmed, a temporary restoration is attached, and the patient leaves with a functional, aesthetic tooth.

Over the following months, the implant undergoes osseointegration — fusing naturally with the surrounding jawbone. Once this process is complete, the temporary restoration is replaced with a high-quality permanent crown, designed to match the colour, shape, and feel of the surrounding teeth.

### Who Is a Good Candidate?

Dr Dmitri Sonin evaluates each patient individually, because candidacy depends on several clinical factors:

- **Sufficient bone volume** at the extraction site to anchor the implant securely
- **Absence of active infection** in the surrounding tissue
- **Good general health**, with no uncontrolled systemic conditions that impair healing
- **Non-smokers or patients willing to stop smoking** during the healing period
- **Healthy gum tissue** around the treatment area

Patients who do not meet these criteria initially are not excluded from implant treatment — they may simply require a preparatory step, such as a bone graft or infection treatment, before proceeding.

### What to Expect After Treatment

The recovery period following immediate implant placement is generally well tolerated. Some swelling, mild tenderness, and sensitivity are normal in the first few days. Patients are advised to follow a soft diet temporarily, maintain careful oral hygiene, and attend scheduled follow-up appointments so the integration process can be monitored closely.

With proper care, dental implants can last a lifetime — making them one of the most worthwhile investments a patient can make in their long-term oral health.
