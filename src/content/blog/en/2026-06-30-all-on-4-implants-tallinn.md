## All-on-4 Dental Implants in Tallinn: A Complete Guide by Dr Dmitri Sonin

### What Are All-on-4 Dental Implants?

All-on-4 is a full-arch dental implant solution that replaces an entire row of missing teeth using just four strategically placed titanium implants. Rather than requiring an individual implant for every missing tooth, two implants are placed vertically at the front of the jaw and two are angled at the back. This angled placement maximises contact with available bone, allowing most patients to receive a fixed, same-day prosthetic bridge — even those who have experienced significant bone loss.

The result is a complete set of teeth that looks natural, feels stable, and functions like real teeth for eating, speaking, and smiling.

### Who Is a Good Candidate?

All-on-4 implants are suitable for adults who:

- Have lost most or all of their teeth in one or both arches
- Wear uncomfortable or ill-fitting dentures
- Have been told they lack sufficient bone for conventional implants
- Want a long-lasting solution that does not rely on adhesives or removable plates

At Sonin Dental Clinic, every patient undergoes a thorough clinical assessment before treatment is recommended. This includes 3D cone beam CT imaging to evaluate bone density, jaw anatomy, and overall oral health. Patients with well-controlled systemic conditions such as diabetes can often still be treated, though each case is assessed individually.

### The All-on-4 Treatment Process at Sonin Dental Clinic

The journey typically begins with a comprehensive consultation where Dr Dmitri Sonin reviews your dental history, imaging results, and aesthetic goals. A personalised treatment plan is then created, outlining each stage of the process.

On the day of surgery, the procedure is carried out under local anaesthesia, with sedation available for anxious patients. Any remaining teeth are extracted if necessary, the four implants are placed, and a temporary fixed bridge is fitted the same day. Patients leave the clinic with a complete smile — not a gap.

After a healing period of several months, during which the implants fuse with the jawbone through a process called osseointegration, the temporary bridge is replaced with a permanent, custom-made prosthesis crafted from high-quality ceramic or zirconia.
