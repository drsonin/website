## All-on-4 Price Timeline in Tallinn: A Complete Patient Guide by Dr Dmitri Sonin

You've spent months researching dental implants, watching your savings, and wondering whether the investment is truly worth it. Maybe you've lost several teeth, or your remaining ones are failing, and someone mentioned All-on-4 as a life-changing solution. Before you commit, you deserve honest, specific answers — not just a brochure. Dr Dmitri Sonin of Sonin Dental Clinic clinic in Tallinn breaks it all down below.

---

### What Exactly Is All-on-4 and Why Does the Price Vary So Much?

All-on-4 is a full-arch restoration technique: four strategically placed implants support an entire set of teeth on one jaw. It's faster, more affordable than individual implants for each tooth, and works even for patients with some bone loss.

So why do quotes range from €6,000 to €18,000+ across Europe? Several factors drive the price:

- **Implant brand** — premium brands (Nobel Biocare, Straumann) cost more but carry proven long-term data
- **Material of the final prosthesis** — temporary acrylic bridges differ significantly from final zirconia arches
- **Clinical complexity** — bone grafting, extractions, or sinus lifts add cost and time
- **Clinic overhead and specialist experience** — Tallinn offers genuinely competitive rates compared to Scandinavia or Western Europe, often 40–60% lower for comparable quality

At Sonin Hambaravi, a full All-on-4 treatment for one jaw using quality implants and a zirconia final prosthesis typically ranges between **€7,500 and €11,000**, depending on your individual case.

### What Does the All-on-4 Price Timeline Actually Look Like, Step by Step?

Understanding *when* you pay what helps you plan realistically. Here is a typical timeline:

**Week 1–2: Consultation and diagnostics (€100–€200)**
A 3D CBCT scan and clinical examination determine bone density, jaw anatomy, and treatment complexity. This fee is often credited toward treatment.

**Week 3–4: Treatment planning and deposit**
A personalised treatment plan is presented with a fixed quote. Most clinics, including ours, request a deposit of 20–30% to reserve your surgery date.

**Day of surgery: Implant placement + temporary bridge (largest payment stage)**
This covers anaesthesia, four implants, any necessary extractions, and a temporary acrylic bridge so you leave with teeth. This typically accounts for 60–70% of the total cost.

**Months 3–6: Healing and monitoring (included in plan)**
Follow-up visits, adjustments to the temporary bridge, and CBCT scans to confirm osseointegration — the process of implants fusing with bone — are part of the programme.

**Month 6–8: Final zirconia prosthesis (remaining balance)**
The permanent bridge is manufactured, fitted, and adjusted. This is the finishing stage that delivers your long-term result.

Total timeline from first consultation to final teeth: **approximately 6–8 months**.

### When Should You Act Quickly and Not Delay?

Dmitri Sonin advises patients not to postpone assessment if they notice:

- **Rapidly progressing bone loss** — visible on X-rays, reducing future implant viability
- **Failing teeth causing infection** — spreading infection complicates implant surgery significantly
- **Dentures that no longer fit** — ongoing bone resorption under loose dentures accelerates bone loss

The longer failing teeth or ill-fitting dentures remain in place, the more bone resorption occurs — and the more complex (and expensive) your eventual treatment becomes. Early action preserves your options and your budget.

---

### How to Prepare and What to Expect Long-Term

**Practical preparation steps:**

1. Get a 3D CBCT scan before any consultation — some clinics offer this free of charge
2. Request an itemised written quote so you can compare like for like
3. Ask specifically: *Which implant brand? What is the final prosthesis material?*
4. Confirm what is included — extractions, bone grafting, and follow-ups should be clearly stated

**Long-term reality:** With proper care — twice-daily brushing, a Water Flosser for cleaning under the bridge, and annual check-ups — All-on-4 implants can last 15–25 years. Dr Dmitri Sonin considers this one of the most reliable and transformative procedures in modern dentistry when done correctly.
