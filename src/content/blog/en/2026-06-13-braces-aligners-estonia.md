### Why Orthodontic Treatment Matters

Misaligned teeth are more than a cosmetic concern. Crowded or crooked teeth can make proper cleaning difficult, increasing the risk of cavities and gum disease. An uneven bite can also cause jaw pain, headaches, and uneven wear on your enamel over time. Whether you're a teenager or an adult, addressing these issues early leads to better long-term oral health outcomes.

Estonia has developed a strong reputation for high-quality, affordable dental care, attracting patients not only from within the country but also from Finland, Sweden, and beyond. ### Types of Braces Available in Estonia

**Traditional Metal Braces**

Metal braces remain one of the most reliable and cost-effective orthodontic solutions. They are particularly well suited for complex cases involving significant crowding, spacing, or bite correction. While they are more visible than other options, modern metal braces are smaller and more comfortable than ever before.

**Ceramic Braces**

For patients who want the effectiveness of traditional braces with a more discreet appearance, ceramic braces are a popular middle ground. The brackets are made from tooth-coloured or clear materials, blending more naturally with the teeth.

**Lingual Braces**

Lingual braces are fitted to the inner surface of the teeth, making them virtually invisible from the outside. This option suits patients who require comprehensive correction but prioritise aesthetics throughout treatment.

### Clear Aligners: A Modern Alternative

Clear aligners have transformed orthodontics over the past decade. These custom-made, removable trays gradually shift teeth into their desired position without the use of brackets or wires. Dmitri Sonin and the team at Sonin Hambaravi offer aligner treatments tailored to each patient's unique dental structure.

**Key advantages of clear aligners include:**

- Near-invisibility during wear
- Removable for eating, drinking, and oral hygiene
- Fewer emergency visits due to broken brackets
- Generally more comfortable than traditional braces

Aligners are best suited for mild to moderate cases of misalignment. During your consultation, Dr Dmitri Sonin will assess your specific needs and recommend the most effective treatment path for you.

### What to Expect During Treatment

Every orthodontic journey begins with a thorough examination, including digital X-rays and, where necessary, 3D imaging. This allows the clinician to map out your bite, jaw structure, and tooth positioning with precision.

Treatment duration varies depending on the complexity of the case. On average, braces are worn for 12 to 24 months, while aligner treatment can range from 6 months to over a year. Regular check-up appointments ensure that progress is on track and any adjustments are made promptly.

After active treatment, a retainer is typically recommended to maintain your new smile long-term. This is an important step that many patients underestimate — keeping the results you've worked hard for.
