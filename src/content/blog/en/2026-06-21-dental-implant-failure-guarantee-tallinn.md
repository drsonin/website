### What Is Dental Implant Failure?

Dental implant failure occurs when the implant does not properly integrate with the jawbone, or when complications arise after what initially seemed like a successful procedure. Failures are generally categorised as:

- **Early failure** — occurring within the first few months, often due to infection, poor bone quality, or the body rejecting the implant
- **Late failure** — developing years after placement, typically caused by gum disease (peri-implantitis), excessive bite force, or underlying health changes

Common warning signs include persistent pain or discomfort, swelling around the implant site, the implant feeling loose, or visible recession of the gum tissue. If you notice any of these symptoms, do not wait — seek professional advice promptly.

### Why Do Implants Sometimes Fail?

Understanding the root causes of failure helps patients and clinicians prevent them. The most frequent contributors include:

- **Insufficient bone density or volume** at the implant site
- **Poor oral hygiene** leading to bacterial infections around the implant
- **Smoking**, which significantly impairs healing and osseointegration
- **Uncontrolled systemic conditions** such as diabetes or osteoporosis
- **Overloading** — placing crowns too early or subjecting implants to excessive chewing forces
- **Low-quality implant components** or inadequate surgical technique

### What Does a Dental Implant Guarantee Cover in Tallinn?

Guarantees for dental implants vary between clinics, so it is important to ask specific questions before committing to treatment. A reliable guarantee should clearly state:

- **Duration** — how many years the implant and the prosthetic crown are covered
- **Conditions** — what patient responsibilities are required to maintain the guarantee (regular check-ups, proper hygiene)
- **What is included** — whether the guarantee covers re-implantation, bone grafting, or only the components themselves

### What to Do If You Suspect Implant Failure

If you are experiencing discomfort or instability with an existing implant — whether it was placed at our clinic or elsewhere — here is what you should do:

1. **Do not ignore the symptoms.** Early intervention significantly improves the chances of saving the implant or successfully replacing it.
2. **Contact your dental clinic immediately** and request an urgent appointment.
3. **Bring any documentation** from your original treatment, including X-rays and treatment records.
4. **Avoid self-medicating** with painkillers alone — they may mask symptoms without addressing the underlying cause.
5. **Get a second opinion** if you feel uncertain about the diagnosis or recommended treatment plan.

### Can a Failed Implant Be Replaced?

In most cases, yes. Once the area has healed — which may take several months — a new implant can often be placed, sometimes combined with a bone grafting procedure. Success rates for replacement implants are comparable to those of first-time placements when risk factors are properly managed.
