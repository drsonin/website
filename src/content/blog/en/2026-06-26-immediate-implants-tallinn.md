## Immediate Dental Implants in Tallinn: What Dr Dmitri Sonin Wants You to Know

### What Are Immediate Dental Implants?

Immediate dental implants — sometimes called same-day implants — are placed directly into the extraction socket on the same day a tooth is removed. Unlike the traditional implant process, which requires a healing period of several months before the implant can be inserted, the immediate protocol combines extraction and implant placement into a single procedure. In many cases, a temporary crown can also be attached on the same day, meaning patients leave the clinic with a functional and natural-looking tooth replacement right away.

This approach saves time, reduces the number of surgical visits, and helps preserve the surrounding bone and gum tissue that can begin to deteriorate shortly after tooth loss.

### How the Procedure Works at Sonin Dental Clinic

At Sonin Dental Clinic, the process begins with a thorough consultation and digital imaging, typically using a 3D cone beam CT scan. This allows the dental team to assess the bone structure, nerve positions, and overall oral health before any treatment begins. Careful planning is what separates a successful immediate implant from a risky one.

On the day of the procedure, the damaged or failing tooth is gently removed, the socket is cleaned, and the titanium implant is precisely placed. Dr Dmitri Sonin and his team take particular care to ensure the implant achieves what is known as primary stability — a secure fit in the bone that is essential for successful osseointegration, the process by which the implant fuses with the jawbone over the following weeks.

A temporary crown or healing cap is then fitted, allowing the patient to eat, speak, and smile with confidence while the implant heals. Once full integration is confirmed, a permanent ceramic crown is placed as the final restoration.

### Who Is a Good Candidate?

Immediate dental implants are not suitable for every patient, and a detailed assessment is always required first. Generally, good candidates are those who:

- Have sufficient bone density and volume at the implant site
- Are in good general and oral health
- Do not smoke heavily or are willing to reduce smoking during healing
- Have no uncontrolled medical conditions such as unmanaged diabetes

Patients with significant bone loss, active infection, or gum disease may need preparatory treatment before an immediate implant is possible. During your consultation at Sonin Dental Clinic, all of these factors are carefully evaluated to ensure the safest and most effective outcome.

### The Long-Term Benefits of Immediate Implants

When placed correctly, dental implants can last a lifetime. They preserve jawbone structure, prevent neighbouring teeth from shifting, and restore full chewing function. Unlike dentures or bridges, implants do not rely on adjacent teeth for support and require no special adhesives or removal at night.

Dmitri Sonin emphasises that long-term success depends not only on the quality of placement but also on good home care and regular professional maintenance. Patients are provided with clear aftercare instructions and scheduled for follow-up appointments to monitor healing and implant stability.
