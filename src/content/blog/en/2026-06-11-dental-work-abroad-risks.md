## Dental Work Abroad: Risks, Guarantees, and the Real Numbers — Insights from Dr Dmitri Sonin

Every year, thousands of patients from Northern Europe travel south for dental treatment. The price difference looks compelling on paper. But before booking a flight to Turkey or Hungary, it is worth running the full numbers — not just the clinic quote.

Dr Dmitri Sonin, implantologist at Sonin Dental Clinic in Tallinn, breaks down the actual costs and risks so patients can make an informed comparison.

---

### The Headline Price vs. the Total Price

A single dental implant with a crown at a clinic in Antalya or Istanbul typically costs **500–800 €**. The same procedure at Sonin Hambaravi in Tallinn is priced at **1,200–1,800 €** per tooth.

That gap — roughly 700–1,000 € per implant — is real. But the total trip cost looks different once travel expenses are added:

| Item | Estimated Cost |
|---|---|
| Implant + crown (Turkey) | 500–800 € |
| Return flights (Tallinn–Antalya) | 200–400 € |
| Hotel (5–7 nights) | 300–600 € |
| Food and local transfers | 150–200 € |
| **Total (single implant)** | **1,150–2,000 €** |

At the upper end of this range, the total cost of dental tourism for one implant already overlaps with local Tallinn prices.

For **two or more implants**, the per-tooth travel overhead decreases, and the foreign option becomes comparatively more cost-efficient — assuming no complications arise.

---

### What Happens When Something Goes Wrong

Implant failure rates in accredited clinics generally fall between 2–5% over ten years. Complications — infection, crown fracture, incorrect bite — can occur regardless of geography. The critical variable is **where you are when it happens**.

If a complication develops after returning to Estonia, the patient faces a straightforward choice: travel back to the original clinic or seek corrective treatment locally.

**Return trip cost estimate:**

- Additional flights: 200–400 €
- Hotel (3–5 nights): 180–350 €
- Corrective procedure: variable, often not covered under foreign warranty terms

A single unplanned return trip adds **380–750 €** or more to the total, before any clinical costs. Two such trips eliminate most or all of the original savings.

Local corrective treatment carries its own complexity. A clinic that did not place the implant may decline responsibility for the outcome, and documentation from abroad is not always complete or compatible with Estonian dental records.

---

### Warranty Terms: Reading the Fine Print

Guarantees vary significantly between providers and countries. Key questions to ask any clinic — domestic or foreign — include:

- What is the exact duration of the warranty?
- Does it cover the implant body, the abutment, and the crown separately?
- Is the warranty transferable if the treating dentist leaves the practice?
- What is the process for filing a claim from another country?

At Sonin Hambaravi, the standard warranty period is **5 years**, with follow-up check appointments typically completed in **30 minutes** at the clinic in Tallinn — no additional travel cost involved.

Foreign clinic warranties often carry similar timeframes on paper, but enforcing them from Estonia requires time, travel, and clear documentation.

---

### Regulatory and Legal Framework

Dental clinics in Estonia operate under EU healthcare regulations and are licensed by the Estonian Health Board (*Terviseamet*). Patients have access to Estonian consumer protection law and the EU cross-border healthcare directive.

Clinics operating in Turkey are regulated under Turkish national law. EU consumer protection rights do not apply. Dispute resolution across jurisdictions is significantly more complex and time-consuming.

This does not mean Turkish clinics deliver lower-quality care — many operate to high standards. It means that legal recourse, if ever needed, follows a different and less accessible path for Estonian residents.

---

### A Structured Way to Compare

Dmitri Sonin recommends patients build a simple decision table before committing to any treatment abroad:

1. Calculate the total trip cost, not the clinic quote alone
2. Add a contingency of at least one return visit (380–750 €)
3. Confirm the warranty terms in writing, including cross-border claim procedures
4. Factor in time off work for travel (typically 7–10 days for implant tourism)
5. Compare that total against the local price, including all follow-up appointments

The numbers will differ for every patient depending on how many implants are needed, travel flexibility, and personal risk tolerance.
