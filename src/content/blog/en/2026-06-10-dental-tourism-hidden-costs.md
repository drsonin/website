## Dental Tourism Hidden Costs: A Real Calculation by Dr Dmitri Sonin

Every year, thousands of patients travel abroad for dental work expecting significant savings. The numbers on paper can look compelling. But a complete financial picture requires accounting for every line item — not just the clinic invoice. Below is a factual cost breakdown to help you make an informed decision.

---

### The Headline Price vs. The Real Price

The most commonly cited comparison involves dental implants with crowns — one of the most frequent procedures patients seek abroad.

**Tallinn (Sonin Dental Clinic clinic):**
- Implant + crown per tooth: **€1,200–€1,800**

**Turkey (Antalya or Istanbul):**
- Implant + crown per tooth: **€500–€800**

The apparent saving per tooth: **€400–€1,300**

That gap is real. The question is what gets added on top.

---

### Travel and Accommodation Costs

Implant treatment typically requires a minimum of two visits: placement and crown fitting. Most clinics abroad consolidate this into one extended trip of five to seven nights.

| Expense | Estimated Cost |
|---|---|
| Return flights Tallinn–Antalya | €200–€400 |
| Hotel (5–7 nights) | €300–€600 |
| Food and local transfers | €150–€200 |
| **Travel subtotal** | **€650–€1,200** |

Adding travel costs to a single-implant procedure in Turkey brings the real total to approximately **€1,150–€2,000** — already overlapping with Estonian pricing before any complications are factored in.

For two or more implants, the travel overhead is spread across multiple units, which improves the per-tooth economics. For a single implant, the margin narrows considerably.

---

### The Complication Variable

Dmitri Sonin, who has been providing implant treatment in Tallinn for over a decade, notes that follow-up care is the most financially unpredictable element of any dental procedure performed abroad.

Implant failure rates vary by provider and patient health, but when complications occur — infection, crown failure, incorrect bite alignment — the patient faces a choice: return to the original clinic or seek treatment locally.

**Cost of a return trip for complications:**
- Additional flights and hotel: **€500–€800**
- This does not include the cost of retreatment itself

Local retreatment in Estonia, performed by a different clinic with no prior records of the case, may also carry a premium.

---

### Warranty and Follow-Up: A Numbers Comparison

Guarantee structures differ significantly between markets.

**Sonin Hambaravi, Tallinn:**
- Implant and crown warranty: **5 years**
- Follow-up check: included, approximately 30 minutes, no additional travel required

**Typical Turkish dental tourism clinic:**
- Warranty terms vary; many are valid only if you return to the original clinic
- Any warranty claim requires travel back to Turkey

The practical value of a guarantee depends entirely on whether exercising it is logistically and financially feasible. A five-year warranty that costs €600 in flights to activate has a different real value than one accessible within your own city.

---

### Scenario Modelling: Single Implant, Two Outcomes

**Scenario A — No complications, Turkey:**
- Implant + crown: €650
- Travel costs: €900 (midpoint estimate)
- **Total: €1,550**

**Scenario B — One complication requiring return trip, Turkey:**
- Implant + crown: €650
- Travel costs: €900
- Return trip for complication: €650
- **Total: €2,200**

**Tallinn baseline:**
- Implant + crown: €1,500 (midpoint)
- Follow-up: included
- **Total: €1,500**

The numbers show that for a single implant, the financial case for travelling abroad depends heavily on whether the treatment proceeds without complications. For multiple implants, the calculation shifts — but complications scale accordingly.

---

### What the Numbers Do Not Capture

This analysis covers direct financial costs only. It does not assign value to time off work, recovery abroad without a support network, language barriers during post-operative care, or the administrative effort of obtaining treatment records from a foreign clinic. Those factors exist but are not quantifiable in a universal way.
