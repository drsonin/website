## Dental Implant Cost in Tallinn: What Dr Dmitri Sonin Wants You to Know

### What Is the Average Cost of a Dental Implant in Tallinn?

In Tallinn, a single dental implant — including the implant fixture, abutment, and ceramic crown — typically ranges from **€900 to €1,800**, depending on the materials used, the complexity of the case, and the clinic's level of expertise. At Sonin Dental Clinic, pricing is transparent and always discussed in full during the initial consultation, so patients are never faced with unexpected costs mid-treatment.

For comparison, the same procedure in Finland, Sweden, or the United Kingdom can easily cost between €2,500 and €4,000 per tooth. This price difference is one of the main reasons Tallinn has become a popular destination for dental tourism across Northern Europe.

### What Factors Affect the Price of Dental Implants?

Several variables influence the total cost of your treatment:

- **Brand and quality of the implant system** — Premium implant brands such as Straumann, Nobel Biocare, or Osstem carry different price points. Dr Dmitri Sonin uses clinically proven implant systems that offer long-term reliability.
- **Bone grafting or augmentation** — If you have experienced bone loss in the jaw, a bone graft may be necessary before the implant can be placed. This adds to the overall cost and treatment timeline.
- **Number of implants** — Full-arch restorations (such as All-on-4 or All-on-6) involve multiple implants and are priced differently from single-tooth replacements.
- **Type of crown or prosthesis** — Zirconia crowns, for example, tend to cost more than standard porcelain-fused-to-metal options but offer superior aesthetics and durability.
- **Imaging and diagnostics** — A 3D CBCT scan is often required to plan implant placement accurately, and this may be included or itemised separately depending on the clinic.

### How Long Does the Implant Process Take?

The full process — from implant placement to fitting the final crown — generally takes between **three and six months**. This timeline allows for osseointegration, the natural process by which the implant fuses with the jawbone. In straightforward cases, same-day or immediate loading implants may be an option, which your clinician will assess during consultation.
