### What Are Wisdom Teeth and Why Do They Cause Problems?

Wisdom teeth typically emerge between the ages of 17 and 25. Because the human jaw has evolved to be smaller over time, there is often not enough space for these late-arriving molars. This can lead to a range of complications, including:

- **Impaction** — the tooth becomes trapped beneath the gum line
- **Crowding** — nearby teeth are pushed out of alignment
- **Infection or pericoronitis** — bacteria collect around partially erupted teeth
- **Cysts or damage** to adjacent teeth and bone

Not every wisdom tooth needs to be removed immediately, but regular dental check-ups help identify problems before they become serious. At Sonin Hambaravi, we use up-to-date diagnostics to assess your individual situation carefully.

### When Should You Consider Wisdom Tooth Removal?

Many patients wonder whether they truly need to remove their wisdom teeth or whether the discomfort will pass on its own. While minor soreness during eruption is normal, certain signs indicate it's time to consult a specialist:

- Persistent pain or pressure at the back of the jaw
- Swelling, redness, or tenderness in the gum tissue
- Difficulty opening your mouth or chewing
- Recurring infections in the same area
- Visible crowding or shifting of other teeth

**Dr Dmitri Sonin** recommends that patients don't wait until the pain becomes unbearable. Early evaluation allows for a more straightforward procedure and a faster recovery.

### Recovery: What Happens After Removal?

Recovery from wisdom tooth removal is usually manageable with proper care. Most patients return to light daily activities within a day or two. Here are some general tips for a smooth recovery:

- Apply a cold pack to your cheek for the first 24 hours to reduce swelling
- Eat soft foods such as yoghurt, soup, and mashed vegetables
- Avoid smoking, alcohol, and using a straw for at least 72 hours
- Rinse gently with warm salt water starting the day after surgery
- Take prescribed or recommended pain relief as directed

Healing is typically complete within one to two weeks, though full bone recovery takes longer. If you experience unusual bleeding, fever, or severe pain, contact the clinic promptly.
