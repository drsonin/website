## Dental Treatment in Tallinn vs Finland: A Cost Comparison by Dr Dmitri Sonin

If you live in Finland and need dental work, you have already noticed the price tags. A single implant with a crown in Helsinki or Tampere can run between €2,500 and €4,000. Many Finnish patients are now looking across the Gulf of Finland — and finding that dental treatment in Tallinn costs significantly less, without the logistical complexity of longer-distance dental tourism.

Dr Dmitri Sonin, founder of Sonin Hambaravi clinic in Tallinn, treats a steady number of Finnish patients each year. This article lays out the numbers so you can make an informed decision.

---

### What Does an Implant Cost in Finland vs Tallinn?

The core procedure most patients compare is a single dental implant with a crown — the standard solution for a missing tooth.

| Location | Implant + Crown (per tooth) |
|---|---|
| Finland (Helsinki area) | €2,500 – €4,000 |
| Tallinn – Sonin Hambaravi | €1,200 – €1,800 |

The price difference per tooth is roughly €1,000 – €2,200. For patients needing multiple implants, the gap compounds quickly. Two implants in Tallinn at the upper estimate (€3,600) still fall below the lower estimate for one implant in Finland (€2,500 × 2 = €5,000).

---

### The True Cost of Travelling to Tallinn from Finland

The ferry from Helsinki to Tallinn takes approximately two hours. Return tickets typically range from €30 to €100 depending on season and booking time. Most implant treatments require two visits: one for implant placement, one for the crown fitting, scheduled three to six months later.

**Estimated total travel cost for two visits:**

- Ferry return tickets × 2 trips: €60 – €200
- Accommodation (1–2 nights per visit, optional): €60 – €160
- Food and transfers: €40 – €80

**Total additional travel cost: approximately €160 – €440**

Adding the maximum travel cost to the upper price of a Tallinn implant still produces a figure (€1,800 + €440 = €2,240) that remains below the minimum Finnish price of €2,500.

---

### How Does This Compare to Dental Tourism in Turkey?

Some patients consider travelling further — to Antalya or Istanbul — where implant and crown packages are advertised at €500 – €800 per tooth. The lower procedure cost is real. However, the total trip cost changes the calculation.

**Estimated costs for one dental trip to Turkey:**

- Return flights from Tallinn: €200 – €400
- Hotel (5–7 nights): €300 – €600
- Food and local transfers: €150 – €200
- **Trip overhead total: €650 – €1,200**

At the lower end, one implant in Turkey plus travel costs approximately €1,150 – €2,000. At the higher end of both ranges, the total reaches €2,000. This is broadly comparable to Tallinn prices — and those figures assume no complications.

**One documented complication requiring a return trip** adds another €500 – €800 in flights and accommodation alone, before any clinical costs. Dmitri Sonin notes that his clinic regularly receives patients who require corrective work after procedures performed abroad, and the clinical and financial costs of revision work are rarely trivial.

At Sonin Hambaravi, the quoted price for an implant and crown includes:

A five-year guarantee with accessible follow-up is a material difference when comparing treatment options. A 30-minute ferry ride for a check-up is not the same as a return flight to another country.
