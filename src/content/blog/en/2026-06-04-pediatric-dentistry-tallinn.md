### Why Children's Dental Care Requires a Specialist Approach

Children are not simply small adults. Their teeth, jaws, and gums are constantly developing, which means they require tailored care that takes growth and age-specific needs into account. Baby teeth may be temporary, but they play a crucial role in speech development, proper chewing, and guiding permanent teeth into correct positions.

Early dental visits also help detect potential issues before they become serious problems. Conditions such as tooth decay, misalignment, or delayed eruption of permanent teeth are far easier — and less costly — to treat when caught early.

### When Should Your Child Have Their First Dental Visit?

Many parents are unsure when to bring their child to the dentist for the first time. The general recommendation is that children should have their first dental check-up around the time their first tooth appears — typically between six and twelve months of age — or no later than their first birthday.

Early visits allow the dentist to assess development, provide guidance on teething, thumb-sucking habits, and diet, and help your child become comfortable with the dental environment from a young age.

### Tips for Maintaining Your Child's Oral Health at Home

Good habits start at home. Dmitri Sonin and our team always encourage parents to:

- Begin brushing as soon as the first tooth appears, using a soft-bristled toothbrush and a rice-grain-sized amount of fluoride toothpaste
- Supervise brushing until the child is around seven or eight years old
- Limit sugary snacks and drinks, especially between meals
- Replace dummies and bottles with cups as early as recommended
- Make brushing fun with songs, timers, or reward charts

These simple steps, combined with regular professional check-ups, give your child the strongest possible foundation for a lifetime of healthy smiles.
