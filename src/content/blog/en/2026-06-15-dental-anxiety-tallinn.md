### What Is Dental Anxiety and Why Does It Happen?

Dental anxiety ranges from mild nervousness before an appointment to intense fear that prevents patients from seeking treatment altogether. Common triggers include fear of pain, loss of control, past traumatic dental experiences, and the sounds or smells associated with a dental clinic.

The consequences of untreated dental anxiety can be serious. Avoiding the dentist leads to worsening oral health, which eventually requires more complex and invasive procedures — creating a cycle that makes the fear even harder to overcome. Recognising this pattern is the first step toward breaking it.

### Techniques Used to Manage Dental Anxiety

There is no one-size-fits-all solution to dental anxiety, which is why the team at Sonin Hambaravi uses a personalised combination of evidence-based strategies.

**Tell-Show-Do Method**
Before any procedure, the dentist explains exactly what will happen, demonstrates the tools involved, and only then proceeds with treatment. This reduces the element of surprise and gives patients a sense of control over the process.

**Agreed Pause Signals**
Patients are encouraged to establish a simple hand signal — such as raising a hand — that immediately pauses the treatment. Knowing you can stop at any moment dramatically reduces feelings of helplessness.

**Sedation Options**
For patients with moderate to severe anxiety, sedation dentistry offers a safe and effective solution. Options may include nitrous oxide (laughing gas), which induces a state of relaxation while keeping you fully conscious, or oral sedation for deeper anxiety relief. Dmitri Sonin carefully evaluates each patient's medical history and anxiety level before recommending any sedation approach.

**Distraction and Environmental Comfort**
Calming music, a friendly tone of voice, and a non-rushed pace all contribute to a more positive experience. Small adjustments in the clinical environment can make an enormous difference for anxious patients.
