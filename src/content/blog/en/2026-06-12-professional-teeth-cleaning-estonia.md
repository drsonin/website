### Why Professional Teeth Cleaning Matters

Over time, minerals in your saliva bind with plaque to form hardened tartar — also known as calculus — which clings to tooth surfaces and accumulates beneath the gumline. Once tartar forms, no amount of home brushing will remove it. Left untreated, it becomes a breeding ground for harmful bacteria, leading to gum disease, bad breath, and eventually tooth loss.

Professional teeth cleaning, known clinically as a **dental prophylaxis**, removes these deposits thoroughly and safely. It also gives your dentist an opportunity to spot early warning signs of cavities, gum inflammation, or other issues before they become costly problems.

### How Often Should You Get Your Teeth Professionally Cleaned?

For most adults, a professional cleaning every **six months** is the standard recommendation. However, some patients benefit from more frequent visits. Smokers, people with diabetes, those prone to heavy tartar build-up, or patients with a history of gum disease may be advised to come in every three to four months.

Dmitri Sonin recommends treating regular cleanings not as a luxury but as a cornerstone of preventive dental care. Catching problems early consistently saves patients both discomfort and expense in the long run.
