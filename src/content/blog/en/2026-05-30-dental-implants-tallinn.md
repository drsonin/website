### What Are Dental Implants?

A dental implant is a small titanium post that is surgically placed into the jawbone, acting as an artificial tooth root. Once integrated with the bone — a process called osseointegration — a custom-made crown is attached on top, creating a result that looks, feels, and functions just like a natural tooth.

Implants are widely considered the gold standard for tooth replacement because they:

- Preserve the jawbone and prevent bone loss
- Do not rely on neighbouring teeth for support
- Can last a lifetime with proper care
- Look and feel completely natural

### Who Is a Good Candidate for Dental Implants?

Most healthy adults are suitable candidates for dental implants. Ideal candidates have:

- Sufficient jawbone density to support the implant
- Healthy gums with no active periodontal disease
- Good general health and no uncontrolled systemic conditions
- A commitment to maintaining good oral hygiene

If bone loss is a concern, procedures such as bone grafting may be recommended before implant placement. This is something the clinic's team can assess and plan for during your initial consultation.

### Caring for Your Dental Implants

One of the great advantages of implants is how easy they are to maintain. Brush twice daily, floss regularly, and attend routine check-ups — essentially the same habits that protect your natural teeth. Avoid smoking, as it can compromise healing and long-term implant success.

With proper care, dental implants can serve you for decades, making them one of the most cost-effective investments you can make in your oral health.
