### Why Do People End Up With No Teeth at All?

Complete tooth loss doesn't happen overnight. It typically results from a combination of factors that compound over years or even decades:

- **Advanced periodontal (gum) disease** — the number one cause of tooth loss in adults over 40. Bacteria destroy the bone and connective tissue that hold teeth in place.
- **Severe untreated decay** — when cavities reach the root and the tooth becomes non-restorable.
- **Trauma or injury** — accidents that fracture teeth beyond repair.
- **Systemic conditions** — diabetes, osteoporosis, and autoimmune disorders can significantly accelerate bone and tooth loss.
- **Long-term neglect or dental anxiety** — one of the most common reasons Dr Dmitri Sonin sees patients who haven't seen a dentist in 10, 15, or even 20 years.

The mechanism is straightforward: once a tooth is lost, the jawbone beneath it begins to shrink — a process called resorption. After just **12 months**, bone volume can decrease by up to 25%. After several years without teeth, there may be significantly less bone available for implants, which is why starting sooner matters.

### What to Do First: The Step-by-Step Starting Point

If you have no teeth — or very few remaining — here is what a realistic treatment journey looks like at Sonin Hambaravi clinic in Tallinn:

**Step 1: Comprehensive consultation and 3D imaging**
The first appointment is not about drilling or discomfort. It's about assessment. Using CBCT (cone beam computed tomography), the team measures exact bone volume, density, and anatomy. This takes about 30–45 minutes.

**Step 2: Diagnosis and treatment planning**
Dmitri Sonin reviews the scan findings and discusses all realistic options with you — including full-arch implant solutions, removable prosthetics, or combined approaches. You receive a written treatment plan with costs and timelines before any work begins.

**Step 3: Choosing your restoration solution**
For completely edentulous (toothless) patients, the most common options are:
- **All-on-4 or All-on-6 implants** — a full arch of fixed teeth supported by 4–6 implants. Temporary teeth can often be fitted on the same day.
- **Implant-retained dentures** — a removable but stable option, typically supported by 2–4 implants.
- **Conventional full dentures** — the fastest and most affordable option, though without implants they don't stop bone loss.

**Step 4: Bone grafting if needed**
If scans reveal insufficient bone, grafting may be required before implant placement. This adds 3–6 months to the timeline but makes implant success rates significantly higher — typically **95–98% over 10 years** with proper care.

### When Should You See a Dentist Immediately?

If you currently have no teeth and experience any of the following, don't wait for a routine appointment:

- **Pain or swelling in the jaw or gums** — could indicate infection even without teeth present
- **Difficulty eating or breathing** — signs of possible systemic issue
- **Your existing denture no longer fits** — accelerating bone loss needs to be evaluated
- **Visible sores or ulcers that don't heal within 2 weeks** — must be checked promptly

### Prevention and Key Takeaways

Once you've committed to treatment, protecting your investment is straightforward. Dr Dmitri Sonin recommends:

- Attending follow-up check-ups every **6 months minimum**
- Cleaning implants and prosthetics daily — plaque still damages implant-supported restorations
- Quitting smoking — it reduces implant success rates by up to **15%**
- Addressing grinding (bruxism) with a night guard if needed

The most important takeaway: **there is no situation too complex to treat**. Patients who've been without teeth for 10+ years are successfully restored every week at clinics like Sonin Hambaravi. The only wrong move is waiting longer.
