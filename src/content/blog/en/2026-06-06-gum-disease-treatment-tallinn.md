### What Is Gum Disease and Why Does It Matter?

Gum disease is a bacterial infection that affects the tissues surrounding and supporting your teeth. It begins as gingivitis — a mild inflammation of the gums — and can progress to periodontitis, a more serious condition that damages the bone and connective tissue holding your teeth in place. Left untreated, periodontitis can lead to tooth loss and has been linked to broader health concerns such as cardiovascular disease and diabetes.

Common warning signs include:

- Gums that bleed when you brush or floss
- Red, swollen, or tender gum tissue
- Receding gums that make teeth appear longer
- Persistent bad breath that does not go away
- Loose or shifting teeth

Early diagnosis and treatment are essential to stopping the disease in its tracks.

### Gum Disease Treatment Options Available in Tallinn

Depending on the severity of your condition, Dmitri Sonin and the clinic team offer a range of proven treatment options:

**Professional Cleaning and Scaling**
For early-stage gum disease, a thorough professional cleaning — including scaling to remove hardened tartar (calculus) from above and below the gum line — is often highly effective. This stops the bacterial build-up that drives inflammation and allows gum tissue to begin healing.

**Root Planing**
In cases of moderate periodontitis, root planing smooths the root surfaces of the teeth, making it harder for bacteria to adhere and giving the gums a clean surface to reattach to. This non-surgical procedure is performed with local anaesthesia to keep you comfortable throughout.

**Antibiotic Therapy**
Localised antibiotic treatments can be used alongside scaling and root planing to target stubborn bacterial colonies deep within periodontal pockets, supporting faster recovery and better long-term outcomes.

**Surgical Periodontal Treatment**
For advanced periodontitis where pockets are deep and bone loss has occurred, surgical options such as flap surgery or bone grafting may be recommended. These procedures aim to restore a healthy gum architecture and preserve as much natural tooth structure as possible.

**Ongoing Maintenance**
Gum disease is a chronic condition, which means that even after successful treatment, regular maintenance appointments are essential. The team at Sonin Hambaravi will create a personalised recall schedule to monitor your gum health and prevent recurrence.
