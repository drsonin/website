## Dental Crown Cost in Estonia: Is It Safe for Finns? — Insights from Dr Dmitri Sonin

Every year, thousands of Finnish patients travel south across the Gulf of Finland to receive high-quality dental care at a fraction of the price they would pay at home. If you have been wondering about dental crown costs in Estonia and whether the journey is truly worth it, this guide will answer your most important questions.

---

### Why Do Finns Travel to Estonia for Dental Crowns?

The price difference between Finnish and Estonian dental care is significant. In Finland, a single porcelain or zirconia dental crown can cost anywhere between €900 and €1,500. In Estonia, the same high-quality crown typically ranges from €250 to €550, depending on the material and complexity of the case.

That gap is not a coincidence — it reflects differences in labour costs, clinic overheads, and healthcare market structures, not differences in quality. Estonian dentists are trained to the same European Union standards as their Finnish counterparts, and many clinics in Tallinn use identical materials, technology, and laboratory partners.

The short ferry ride from Helsinki to Tallinn (roughly two to two and a half hours) makes the trip genuinely convenient. Many patients combine their dental appointment with a pleasant day or weekend in the city.

### Is It Actually Safe? What You Need to Know

Safety is the most important question any patient should ask before travelling abroad for dental treatment. The honest answer is yes — provided you choose a reputable, experienced clinic.

Estonia is a European Union member state, which means dental clinics operate under EU health and safety regulations, the same regulatory framework that governs Finnish dentistry. Dentists must hold recognised qualifications, and clinics are subject to inspection and licensing requirements.

### What Types of Dental Crowns Are Available in Tallinn?

Patients visiting Sonin Hambaravi can choose from several crown options, each suited to different clinical needs and budgets:

- **Zirconia crowns** — the most popular choice for their strength, natural appearance, and biocompatibility
- **Porcelain-fused-to-metal (PFM) crowns** — a reliable, cost-effective option for back teeth
- **Full ceramic crowns** — excellent aesthetics, ideal for front teeth
- **Temporary crowns** — placed while the permanent crown is being fabricated

During your consultation, Dmitri Sonin will evaluate your existing tooth structure, bite, and aesthetic goals before recommending the most appropriate material.

### What Does the Treatment Process Look Like for Finnish Patients?

Most dental crown procedures at the clinic require two visits, which can often be scheduled within the same trip or across two separate visits a week or two apart.

1. **First appointment:** Examination, X-rays, tooth preparation, and digital impression
2. **Laboratory phase:** Your crown is fabricated — typically within five to ten working days
3. **Second appointment:** Crown fitting, adjustments, and final placement

For patients who cannot return easily for a second visit, the clinic can coordinate with Finnish dental colleagues for final placement, or arrange an extended stay when necessary.

### Practical Considerations Before You Travel

Before booking your trip, keep these points in mind:

- Bring any recent dental X-rays or records you already have
- Allow sufficient time — avoid rushing between the ferry and your appointment
- Check whether your Finnish dental insurance or Kela reimbursement applies to treatment received in an EU member state
- Ask the clinic for a detailed treatment plan and cost estimate in writing before you travel
